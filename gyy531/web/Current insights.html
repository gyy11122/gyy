Sketching is a very basic form of painting - it reflects the fundamental principles of painting by depicting objects in a certain way.
The distinction between black, white, and gray is based on the position of the light source and the object's inherent color.
Sketching breaks down the various shapes and colors of the three-dimensional world into a series of long or short, deep or shallow lines - much like how we used velocity-time graphs to find displacement in middle school,
in essence converting complex relationships into very simple lines.

Today, I am very grateful for my persistence and efforts over the years, which gave me the opportunity to slow down in today's fast-paced life, and gave me the opportunity to express my understanding of beauty with the relationship between black and white and gray.
